# Spaces and Organization

Spaces are the top-level containers in Tessera. Each space holds its own notes, tasks, members, and permissions, which keeps personal work and team work cleanly separated.

## Types of space

- **Personal space** — created automatically with your account. Only you can see it unless you share an individual note. You always have exactly one personal space and it cannot be deleted.
- **Shared spaces** — created for a team, project, or topic. Members you invite can see everything in the space according to their role.

## Creating and naming spaces

Create a space from the **+** button next to **Spaces** in the sidebar. A space has a name, an icon, and an optional one-line description shown on its home page. Names must be unique within your account and can be up to 60 characters.

On the **Free** plan you can have up to **2 shared spaces**. The **Plus**, **Team**, and **Business** plans allow unlimited shared spaces.

## Folders and nesting

Inside a space, organize notes with **folders**. Folders can be nested up to **8 levels** deep. Drag a note onto a folder to move it, or use **Move to…** from the note's **⋯** menu.

A note lives in exactly one folder at a time. To make a note reachable from several places, use links (`[[Note name]]`) rather than copies — this keeps a single source of truth.

## Pinning and favorites

Pin up to **15 notes or folders** to the top of the sidebar for quick access. Pins are personal: pinning a note only affects your own sidebar, not your teammates'.

## Sorting and views

Each folder can be sorted by **Title**, **Last edited**, **Created date**, or **Manual** order. Manual order lets you drag notes into a custom sequence. The sort choice is saved per folder and shared with everyone in the space.

## Archiving

When a project ends, **archive** the space instead of deleting it. Archived spaces are hidden from the sidebar but remain fully searchable and can be restored at any time from **Settings → Spaces → Archived**. Archiving does not count against your active-space limit on the Free plan.

## Moving notes between spaces

Use **Move to…** and pick a destination space. Moving a note also moves its comments and version history. Links pointing to the moved note are updated automatically so they never break.
