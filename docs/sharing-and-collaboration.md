# Sharing and Collaboration

Tessera is built for working together. You can share an entire space, a single note, or a read-only public link, and edit alongside teammates in real time.

## Real-time editing

When two or more people open the same note, you see each other's **cursors** and **selections** with name labels, and edits appear instantly. There is no locking and no merge step — Tessera resolves concurrent edits automatically.

## Permission roles

Access is controlled by four roles, which apply at the space level and can be overridden on an individual note:

- **Viewer** — can read content but not change it.
- **Commenter** — can read and add comments, but not edit content.
- **Editor** — can read, comment, and edit content.
- **Admin** — full Editor rights plus the ability to manage members and space settings.

Set a person's role when you invite them, or change it later from **Space settings → Members**.

## Inviting people

Invite teammates by email from **Space settings → Members → Invite**. Invitation links expire after **7 days**. On the **Free** plan a shared space allows up to **3 members**; the **Team** and **Business** plans allow up to **250 members** per space.

## Guests

A **guest** is someone added to specific notes or spaces but not a full member of your organization. Guests are available on the **Team** and **Business** plans and are useful for clients or contractors. Guests only see what they're explicitly shared and never appear in your member directory.

## Public links

Publish a note to the web with **Share → Create public link**. Anyone with the link can read it without a Tessera account. Public links are **read-only**, can be password-protected, and can be set to expire on a chosen date. Disable a public link at any time to revoke access immediately.

Public links never expose other notes, your space, or your member list — only the single published note.

## Mentions and handoffs

Type `@` to mention a teammate in a note or comment. They receive a notification in their Inbox with a direct link to the spot you mentioned them, which makes handing off work quick and traceable.

## Sharing a single note

To share one note from an otherwise private space, open **Share** on the note and add people directly. Note-level sharing grants access only to that note, not the surrounding space.
