# Version History

Tessera automatically saves snapshots of every note so you can review how it changed and roll back if needed. You never lose work to an accidental edit.

## How snapshots are captured

A new **version** is captured automatically when a note has been idle for a few minutes after edits, and at least every hour during active editing. Each version records the author, a timestamp, and the full content at that moment. You can also create a manual snapshot at any time with **Save version** from the note's **⋯** menu.

## How long versions are kept

Retention depends on your plan:

- **Free** — 7 days of version history
- **Plus** — 90 days of version history
- **Team** — unlimited version history
- **Business** — unlimited version history

Versions older than your plan's window are permanently deleted and cannot be recovered.

## Reviewing versions

Open the **Version history** tab in the right context panel, or press **Cmd/Ctrl + Shift + H**. The timeline lists every snapshot newest-first. Select a version to preview it; additions are highlighted in green and deletions in red so changes are easy to scan.

## Restoring a version

With a version selected, click **Restore**. The note's current content is replaced by that snapshot — but the act of restoring itself creates a new version, so nothing is ever lost and you can always undo a restore.

## Named versions

Click the label on any snapshot to give it a name such as "Approved draft" or "Before redesign." Named versions are pinned to the top of the timeline and are exempt from automatic cleanup, even on the Free plan.

## Per-block authorship

Hover any block and choose **Block history** to see who last edited just that block and when. This is useful in shared spaces for understanding who changed a specific paragraph without diffing the whole note.

## Boards and tasks

Version history applies to notes. Tasks keep a separate **activity log** showing status changes, reassignments, and due-date edits, visible in the task's detail panel.
